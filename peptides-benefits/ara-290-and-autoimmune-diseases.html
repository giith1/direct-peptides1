Understanding Autoimmune Diseases

Autoimmune diseases are like a well-meaning but overzealous security system that mistakes friendly visitors for intruders. Instead of protecting the body, the immune system launches an attack on its own cells, leading to chronic inflammation and tissue damage.

There are more than 80 known autoimmune disorders, each affecting different organs and systems. Some of the most common include:

  • Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): The immune system targets the joint linings, causing pain and swelling.

  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS): This condition affects the central nervous system, leading to nerve damage and loss of coordination.

  • Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus: Immune cells attack insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.

  • Lupus (SLE): A systemic disease where the immune system attacks various organs, including the skin, kidneys, and heart.
