Research indicates that the primary function of FTPP Adipotide is to aid in weight loss, with the substance demonstrating promising results in various tests. Adipotide is designed to target specific blood vessels that supply fat (adipose tissue), leading to the constriction of these vessels. As a result, the fat cells begin to break down and consume these blood vessels.

This process triggers apoptosis, a form of programmed cell death (PCD) that typically occurs in multicellular organisms. Adipotide binds to two key receptors, prohibitin and ANXA2, which are located in blood vessels and play a role in supplying white fat tissue. These receptors are found in capillaries and are crucial for maintaining the function of white fat tissue.

FTPP (Adipotide) is a peptidomimetic, a small protein-like chain designed to mimic a peptide, and it has been developed as a proapoptotic agent to induce cell death. This mechanism enhances its ability to promote weight loss.

Adipotide has been tested in a Phase 1 clinical trial as a potential anti-obesity treatment, demonstrating its promise for medical applications. In one study, the administration of Adipotide to obese rhesus monkeys resulted in significant fat loss and improved insulin resistance by targeting and inducing apoptosis in the blood vessels of white adipose tissue.
