Mechanism of Action of CJC-1295 DAC

CJC-1295 DAC is a modified growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog, comprising 30 amino acids. It works by binding to receptors in the anterior pituitary gland, stimulating the release of growth hormone (GH). This interaction prompts a pulsatile release of GH, mimicking the body’s natural secretion rhythm.

The inclusion of a drug affinity complex (DAC) significantly extends the peptide’s half-life, enabling prolonged GH release over extended periods.

When administered, CJC-1295 DAC temporarily enhances GH secretion, leading to elevated serum GH levels and changes in serum protein profiles, including fluctuations in serum albumin. Unlike exogenous GH therapies, this mechanism relies on the stimulation of the body’s endogenous GH production.

Official FAQ
1. Is CJC-1295 DAC the same as CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) is a modified version of the peptide CJC-1295. The main difference lies in the addition of the Drug Affinity Complex, which significantly extends its half-life compared to the original CJC-1295 (also known as Mod GRF 1-29).

This modification allows CJC-1295 DAC to provide a sustained release of growth hormone, making it potentially better suited for certain therapeutic applications or research objectives. In contrast, the shorter half-life of CJC-1295 without DAC creates a more natural, short-lived pulse of growth hormone, which some researchers believe mimics the body’s natural rhythm more closely.

2. What is the half-life of CJC-1295 DAC?

The half-life of CJC-1295 DAC is substantially longer than its non-DAC counterpart, lasting approximately 5 to 8 days. This extended half-life reduces the frequency of dosing, improving convenience and potentially enhancing compliance in research settings.

3. Is CJC-1295 DAC legal?

The legality of CJC-1295 DAC varies by jurisdiction. In many countries, including the United States, it is classified as a research chemical and is not approved for human consumption. It is intended strictly for scientific research purposes.

Researchers and buyers must ensure compliance with local laws and regulations before purchasing or using CJC-1295 DAC.
